I am a sales rep and given is a transcript between me and a prospect.

# GOAL
Your goal is to evaluate my performance as a sales rep by analyzing both the content of what I said and how I communicated it, including:
- confidence, professionalism, and rapport-building
- discovery and needs assessment quality
- objection handling effectiveness
- closing technique and next-step clarity

# EVALUATION GUIDELINES
When reviewing the call, assess me on:
- **Opener & Rapport**: Did I open strong? Did I build rapport naturally or jump straight into pitching?
- **Discovery**: Did I ask thoughtful questions to understand the prospect's pain points, budget, timeline, and decision process?
- **Objection Handling**: When the prospect pushed back, did I acknowledge, empathize, and reframe effectively? Or did I dismiss, argue, or fold?
- **Value Articulation**: Did I connect our solution to the prospect's specific needs? Or was my pitch generic?
- **Closing & Next Steps**: Did I propose a clear next step? Was my close confident without being pushy?
- **Listening**: Did I actually listen to the prospect, or did I talk over them and miss signals?

# SCORING
Rate each dimension on a scale of 1-10:
1. Opener & Rapport (1-10)
2. Discovery (1-10)
3. Objection Handling (1-10)
4. Value Articulation (1-10)
5. Closing & Next Steps (1-10)
6. Active Listening (1-10)
Overall Score: (average, 1-10)

# OUTPUT FORMAT
Provide feedback in the following structure:
- Use second person ("you") to address me directly.
- Start with the numerical scores for each dimension.
- Then provide a bullet-point list of specific, actionable areas for improvement.
- Each point must reference a specific moment in the conversation.
- Do not soften or sugar-coat the feedback. If the performance was poor, say it plainly.
- End with 1-2 things I did well (if any).
- Use stars ("*") for your bullet points.
